Youth Serving Christ (YSC)

The Parish Youth Serving Christ is a Catholic Youth group that brings together youth from the three different outstations to evangelize and grow together in living their Christian lives. The purpose and direction of the group is summarized in its motto; together we journey together we serve.

MEMBERSHIP
The members of the Youth Serving Christ are the active and fully registered youth in all outstations. Membership is also open to unmarried youth between 18 and 27 years. To be fully registered, a youth has to present copies of the national identification card, baptismal card, letter from the respective Small Christian Community where they belong, two passport-sized photos.

ACTIVITIES
The main role of the Youth Serving Christ in the parish is liturgical; singing in the mass. Our activities are categorized into two groups; spiritual and social. Spiritually, the group engages in recollections, group prayers, weekly Bible sharing, spiritual talks as well as charity works. Socially, the Y.S.C engages in sporting tournaments and cultural events dubbed ‘African Sunday’. We also have recreational activities such as hiking, sports, study tours and other bonding activities.

FAITH AND CHRISTIAN LIVING
The Youth Serving Christ impacts faith in its entire membership by having spiritual talks on a monthly basis and having recollections often. In addition, the spiritual animators who journey with us are helpful in making faith-based decisions regarding matters. By serving together, Christian living is impacted in the fact that we are able to make social friendships that are based on our faith.

  • Holy mass animation deepens our faith living
  • Refresher of catechism classes to understand our faith as Catholics
  • Through Bible trivias enhances our faith living
  • More awareness and learning of our patron saints (St Francis of Assis ,St Vincent Pallotti and Sacred Heart of Jesus ) enriches our faith building
  • After Holy mass animation Sundays/Weekly prayers of Divine Mercy Chaplet ,Holy Rosary acts as an enlightenment to young persons Catholic faith.
  • Attending of Small Christian community (SCC) helps youth to journey together with rest of Christian for evangelization
