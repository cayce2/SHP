Legion of Mary

PRAESIDIUM: Our Lady of Lourdes, Corner The Legion of Mary is an Association of Catholics who, with the sanction of the church and under the powerful leadership of Mary Immaculate, Mediatrix of all Graces, have formed themselves into a legion for service in the warfare which is waged by the church against the world and its evil powers.

It is organized on the model of the Roman army and adopts its terminology. It is at the disposal of the bishop diocese and the parish priest for any and every form of social service and catholic action which they deem suitable to the legionaries and useful for the warfare of the church. The legionaries have a duty to show whole-hearted devotion to Mother Mary by serious meditation and Zealous practice. The Legionary service is a lifelong perseverance in the work of the apostolate. Legionaries knowing themselves to be soldiers are stimulated to impart a soldierly seriousness to their work. We meet on Saturdays in the church compound (tent) at 7:10 am after morning mass.

ACTIVITIES

  • Sanctification of the Legionaries through prayer and participation in the mission of evangelization by direct apostolate among those who are far from church, home visits to the sick and needy families.
  • Teaching catechism
  • Religious instruction for young people
  • Supporting drug addicts and street children
  • Visiting hospitals and praying for the sick
  • Visiting inmates
  • Visiting cemeteries ; praying for the souls in purgatory
  • Praying for priests and religious

    • MEMBERSHIP
    Legion of Mary is open to all Catholics who faithfully practice their religion , want to fulfill their role in the church’s apostolate through Legion and are prepared to fulfill each and every duty which active membership of the Legion involves. It takes a candidate three months to be enrolled in the ranks of the Legionaries. When probation period is satisfactorily completed, the candidate is given a week’s notice of reception to familiarize with the words and the ideas of the Legionary promise.
