CJPC

Introduction

Catholic justice and peace commission is an organization of the church that was established by the KCCB in 1988 and it's main aim is to awaken God's people to a full understanding of justice and peace and also raise awareness sensitize and empower individuals, groups, communities and the whole nation on justice, peace issues their respective roles and duties in addition to peace development, human advancement and human rights in light of the gospel and social teaching of the catholic church.

Membership

Members shall be:-
  • Baptized and have clear understanding of the catholic faith.
  • Christian endowed with integral morals and proven virtues.
  • Be Just person and a role model.
  • Christian who is committed towards justice in the society.
  • Be Courageous at the time of temptation and stand firm to what is just not withstanding the consequences.
  • Be Non-judgmental.
  • Be Non-patrician especially in conflict management and resolution.
  • Be a person of great integrity.
  • God fearing and ready to suffer for what they believe to be just and right in defense of justice and peace for all, especially the weak, poor and oppressed.

    • Activities

    Cjpc activities are as follows:-
  • Empower members of Scc with knowledge of peace and justice.
  • Educate people through transformative civil education in workshops and training.
  • Protection of human dignity according to social teaching of catholic church
  • Facilitate process of reconciliation between people in the conflict.
  • To network and build partnerships with like-minded organization in pursuit of human rights injustice.

    • Contributing towards good governance by providing integrated and ongoing civil education which includes:-
  • Good and responsible citizenship
  • Voters registration
  • Voters education
  • Monitoring of election o Constitutional review.
  • Help to identify and support needy people in the SCCs.
  • Lead SCCs in the Lenten Campaign and resource mobilization.
  • Help to keep peace and security at the SCCs and the neighborhoods.

    • Faith formation

  • Through sensitizing, animating, mobilizing and lobbying the parishioners to respond to social responsibility according to the social teaching of the Catholic Church.
  • Through teaching, creating awareness, capacity building, networking and caucuses among the parishioners.
  • Through seminars, retreat and recollection.

    • Recruitment of new members.

    Members are recruited /elected or nominated from each Small Christian Community (SCCs), ecclesial groups, devotional groups, movements and religious institution at the local church.
