Catholic Women Association

Catholic Women Assocaition (CWA) is an association of catholic lay women founded in the year 1980 during the visit of Pope John 11to kenya under the guidance of the late Servant of God Cardinal Maurice Otunga. Its Main goal is to enhance spiritual growth of Catholic Women and enable them participate fully and more effectively in evangelization and promoting Socio Economic well-being among Members.

Vision :To seek and bear witness to christ Committed to holistic development of families being the being the first Christine church

Patron Saint: St. Monica

Motto: Strong in Faith

Membership:

A woman who embrace the Catholic Faith baptized and has attained 18years and above and have gone through formation classes, commissioned and also who take and renew their promises to be a woman of prayers like their patron St. Monica guided by three pillars: Catholic Faith,Members united in purpose and visionary Leadership. Recruitment of Members is done on yearly basis through parish announcements and verification on baptismal checking and confirmation from Small Christian Community leaders on member participation in the SCC through filling of a designed form. Our Major activities is enhancing spiritual growth through praying together in our meetings, retreats, recollection, seminars and sharing the word of God.
  • Church Development at all Church levels, SCC, Parish activities, Deanery and Archdiocese level.
  • Helping the needy and the sick
    • We impact faith and Christians living in the Parish by working with the church Leadership in the Apostolic work to ensure we meet the 10 years strategic plan and other Church development.
  • Bringing women together so that they can effectively participate in the general development of the church and promote Sociol economic wellbeing
  • During Our Harvest Month in October, we bring food to assist the needy members within the parish
  • Visiting and praying with the needy within Our Parish
